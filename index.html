Abstract

Multimodal data is available in many applications like e-commerce production listings, social media posts and short videos. However, existing algorithms dealing with those types of data still focus on uni-modal representation learning by vision-language alignment and cross-modal retrieval. With the popularity of large langauge models (LLMs), some works try to employ an LLM's reasoning capability for vision based tasks like VQA. Those tasks are generation based but not retrieval tasks. In this workshop, we target to bring a new retrieval problem where both queries and documents are multimodal. We will show the uniqueness of the problem and the new challenges. Many industrial search engines include Google Lens and Amazon Lens have launched multimodal search features. However, there are very few papers/workshops covering this topic. This event will be a comprehensive half-day workshop focusing on the subject of multimodal representation and retrieval. The agenda includes keynote speeches, detailed oral presentations, and an interactive panel discussion.
